# Plunger Sub Assembly

## Steps

### Step 1
The Plunger Sub-Assembly is the half of the core that stores energy and is released upon firing, pushing air down the plunger tube to propel the dart. It slides inside the plunger tube and along the barrel, and is the assembly with the most impact on the performance and efficiency of the blaster.

![Step 1 image 1](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-01_picture1.png)

### Step 2
Take the PlungerHead, CatchEnd, and SpringBase (first photo, second photo, third photo respectively) and clean out their respective center through holes (closeup of PlungerHead center section in fourth photo). These inner sections  will slide on the Barrel/CatchTube, and so minimizing friction is key for performance and consistency. Remove any stray plastic, and sand out any inconsistencies if needed. If desired, you can lightly sand the inside with some fine sandpaper, though be sure not to fully sand out the ridges as these are key to minimizing friction and clearing out debris.

### Step 3
Test fit to make sure the PlungerHead and CatchEnd slide freely on the Barrel, and the SpringBase slides freely on the CatchTube (bottom photos). If they do not slide freely, sand out the inside until they slide with minimal resistance. Note: PlungerHead has an o-ring that it primarily slides on, so the inner ridges should be a looser fit. CatchEnd and SpringBase will directly slide on these ridges, so they should be able to slide freely but not be overly loose. See tuning notes for more in depth information about optimization and fitment.

![Step 3 image 1](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-03_picture1.png)
![Step 3 image 2](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-03_picture2.png)

### Step 4
Take the CatchTube and insert either end (it is symmetric) into the PlungerHead (first photo). It should be a relatively snug fit. Carefully rotate it until the holes align, it may take some effort due to the snug fit (second photo), then confirm that all four holes line up well. If these do not align, the PlungerHead may not be able to attach well to the CatchTube, and a new CatchTube may be needed. Then, rotate the CatchTube again until the holes are hidden (this is to provide a base for the heat set insert in the next step, and to prevent molten plastic from getting into the CatchTube holes).

![Step 4 image 1](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-04_picture1.png)
![Step 4 image 2](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-04_picture2.png)
![Step 4 image 3](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-04_picture3.png)

### Step 5
Carefully install (4) 6-32  ¼” Heat Set Inserts into the four PlungerTube holes (first photo), pushing until the insert is up against the CatchTube (cutaway in second photo). Once the heat set inserts cool, confirm the CatchTube rotates, and confirm its holes now align with the heat set inserts (third photo). Remove and set aside the CatchTube. Place a 127 O-Ring around the PlungerHead, and an 016 O-Ring inside (fourth photo). Take another 127 O-Ring, and thread it back and forth across the front cutouts of the PlungerHead to act as a bumper (fifth photo).

### Step 6
Note: If using the optional TPU Bumper use this instead of the second 127 O-Ring.

### Step 7
WARNING: Incorrectly installing the screws in this step can permanently damage the CatchTube, which may require replacing. Be very careful to only thread the screws into the holes of the CatchTube, and do not overtighten which may strip the threading.

### Step 8
Insert the CatchEnd onto the CatchTube, being careful to align the holes (first photo). Carefully screw in (4) 6-32 ⅛” Socket Head Screws into the four holes (second photo), tightening until flush and secure (third photo). Loctite is recommended. Confirm the CatchEnd still slides freely over the Barrel (fourth photo). If it does not, check to make sure the screws are correctly threaded into the CatchTube (otherwise they may just be pushing against the CatchTube compressing it without securely fastening). If the screws are correctly threaded and it is still too tight to slide freely, lightly sand out the inside of the CatchEnd until the Barrel can slide freely without being overly loose.

### Step 9
Slide the SpringBase onto the CatchTube, making sure the flat diagonal side with the rectangular holes is facing the CatchEnd and the side with the rounded recess is facing out (first photo). Rotate the SpringBase until its angled face aligns with the angled face of the CatchEnd (second photo).

![Step 9 image 1](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-09_picture1.png)
![Step 9 image 2](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-09_picture2.png)

### Step 10
Slide the Spring onto the CatchTube (first photo), making sure the base of the spring fits cleanly into the groove of the SpringBase (second photo).

![Step 10 image 1](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-10_picture1.png)
![Step 10 image 2](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-10_picture2.png)

### Step 11
Warning: Incorrectly installing the screws in this step can permanently damage the CatchTube, which may require replacing. Be very careful to only thread the screws into the holes of the CatchTube, and do not overtighten which may strip the threading. The heat set inserts in the PlungerHead help prevent overtightening and allow repeated removal/installation, though still be careful as missing the holes can dent the CatchTube.

![Step 11 image 1](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-11_picture1.png)
![Step 11 image 2](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-11_picture2.png)
![Step 11 image 3](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-11_picture3.png)
![Step 11 image 4](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-11_picture4.png)

### Step 12
Place the PlungerHead on the end of the CatchTube, making sure to push it on as far as it will go and rotating until the holes align (top photo). Carefully screw in (4) 6-32 ⅝”  Phillips Screws, tightening until secure without overtightening (top right photo). Loctite is recommended. I also recommend first loosely installing a single screw, and confirming the remaining holes still align before tightening and installing the remaining screws. Make sure the screws are installed far enough that they are below the 127 O-Ring to prevent scraping in the plunger tube (bottom left photo). Confirm the SpringBase can move freely along the CatchTube, compressing the Spring (bottom right photo).

### Step 13
Apply some Lube to the inner 016 O-Ring of the PlungerHead (first photo), then confirm the entire assembly moves freely on the Barrel (second photo).

![Step 13 image 1](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-13_picture1.png)
![Step 13 image 2](../../media/core/plunger-sub-assembly-step-13_picture2.png)

### Step 14
Note: It may take a little sliding and rotating before the lube disperses enough along the barrel to move easily. If the PlungerHead is too tight, it may indicate the screws missed the holes and are instead compressing the CatchTube without proper fastening. If after checking the screws the problem persists, you can remove the inner 016 O-Ring, check the inside of the PlungerHead, and sand down the inside until the barrel slides freely. Alternatively, you may need to adjust for printer tolerances of the inner 016 O-Ring groove to allow for smoother movement
