# Gear Tensioner Assembly

[View Materials List](materials.md)

## Steps

### Step 1
FrontConnectorCap (1), FrontConnectorBase, Your chosen Shroud (Railgun style here)

![Step 1 image 1](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-01_picture1.png)

### Step 2
LockBlockCap, TensionerBlock, 2x LockBlock, NutCap, BarrelGear, 2x SideGear, OuterGear, InnerGear

### Step 3
2x 3/4th in 3/32” dowel, 2x 10-32 nut, 1x 3-32 by 6mm heat set insert, 1x washer, 2x 6-32 nut, 1x 1-1/4th in Oval Head Screw, 2x 5/8in 6-32 socket head bolt, 4x 6-32 5/16th screw, 2x 6-32 thumbscrews.

### Step 4
Warning: This section requires glueing, read the entire instruction before starting as you will have limited time to complete before the superglue sets.

![Step 4 image 1](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-04_picture1.png)
![Step 4 image 2](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-04_picture2.png)
![Step 4 image 3](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-04_picture3.png)

### Step 5
Insert the 1 1/4th in 10-32 screw into the InnerGear on the circular side (first photo). Screw on the 10-32 nut until it fits into the cutout on the bottom of the InnerGear, leaving the screw slightly out (first and second photo). Apply a small drop of super glue to the underside of the screw head and top of its threads, then quickly screw it in until it is fully against the InnerGear and the nut on the other side is snug (third photo). Wipe off any excess glue, then allow 15 minutes to dry.

### Step 6
Place FrontConnectorCap on top of your chosen Shroud (first photo). Attach the two with 2x 5/16th screws in the inner two bottom holes, then apply some lube to the bottom where the gears will rest (second photo). Insert the BarrelGear, large side down in the top compartment (third photo). Insert two SideGear into their respective side cutout, small side down, with a 3/4th inch dowel in each (fourth photo). Place the OuterGear in the outer cutout as well (fourth photo). Finally, place the InnerGear with its glued in screw in the bottom cutout, then place the washer on top (fifth photo).

### Step 7
Take the TensionerBlock and insert a 10-32 nut in the compartment (first photo). Take the NutCap and insert the split flanged side first until it clicks into the grooves on the left and right above the nut (second and third photo). Test that the pin fits easily through the hole across the entire block, then remove (fourth photo).

![Step 7 image 1](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-07_picture1.png)
![Step 7 image 2](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-07_picture2.png)
![Step 7 image 3](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-07_picture3.png)
![Step 7 image 4](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-07_picture4.png)

### Step 8
Take the FrontConnectorBase and insert a 6-32 heat set into the top hole within the rectangular block (first and second photo). Push in as straight as possible until flush with a soldering iron set to the appropriate temperature for your print (third photo), then trim any excess (fourth photo).

![Step 8 image 1](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-08_picture1.png)
![Step 8 image 2](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-08_picture2.png)

![Step 8 image 4](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-08_picture4.png)

### Step 9
In this step we will be placing the FrontConnectorBase on top of the FrontConnectorCap assembly. There are a number of pieces that must fit together, so make sure you understand everything that must align before pushing the piece into place. The left and right dowels fit into corresponding holes(first photo, red and green arrows). The InnerGear’s screw goes through the larger center hole (first photo blue arrow). Finally make sure the top left and top right grooves align (second photo). Push FrontConnectorBase snugly into place once you verify all the alignments (third photo).

![Step 9 image 1](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-09_picture1.png)
![Step 9 image 2](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-09_picture2.png)
![Step 9 image 3](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-09_picture3.png)

### Step 10
Screw the Shroud into the heat set insert in FrontConnectorBase with a 5/16th screw from the top (first photo). Use two 5/8th socket head bolts to screw FrontConnectorCap into FrontConnectorBase from the shroud side, being careful not to overtighten (second photo blue arrows). Next, flip the assembly back over and insert two 6-32 nuts into the little slots on the left and right of FrontConnectorBase (third photo red arrows). Additionally, insert both LockBlocks into their grooves, with the tapered side down (third photo green arrows). Place the LockBlockCap on top into the fitting cutout, and use a 5/16th screw to screw it into place (fourth photo).

![Step 10 image 1](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-10_picture1.png)
![Step 10 image 2](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-10_picture2.png)
![Step 10 image 3](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-10_picture3.png)
![Step 10 image 4](../../media/gear-tensioner/gear-tensioner-step-10_picture4.png)

### Step 11
Place the TensionerBlock into the bottom of the assembly with the smaller side face up, making sure it aligns with the cutout behind (first photo). Additionally, take two 6-32 thumb screws and insert them into the holes on the top left and right of the assembly until they screw into the captive nuts above the LockBlocks (first and second photo blue arrows). Turn the OuterGear on the side to pull the TensionerBlock into place until the hole aligns with the cutout (second photo red arrow). The bolt should be roughly flush with the end of the TensionerBlock (third photo). Finally, test that the inserted thumbscrews when tightened push the LockBlocks into the center where the barrel will ultimately go, locking the barrel into place (fourth photo green arrows). Back off the thumbscrews to allow a barrel to be inserted later.
